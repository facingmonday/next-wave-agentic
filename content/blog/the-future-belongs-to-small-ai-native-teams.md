A recent message from Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong stood out because it put language around something a lot of founders, operators, and business owners are already starting to feel.

The way companies work is changing.

Armstrong described a future where Coinbase becomes leaner, flatter, faster, and more AI-native. He pointed to fewer layers, more player-coach leaders, smaller teams, and even "one person teams" where engineers, designers, product managers, and AI agents work together to create outsized impact.

That is not just a Coinbase story.

That is where business is headed.

And for us at Next Wave Agentic, it reinforces something we have believed for a while:

> The future does not belong only to the biggest companies. It belongs to the smallest teams with the smartest systems.

## AI Is Changing What Small Teams Can Do

For a long time, growth usually meant adding more people.

If a company needed more marketing, it hired a bigger marketing team. If it needed more software, it hired more developers. If it needed more content, it hired writers, designers, editors, videographers, and coordinators.

That model still has its place, but AI is changing the equation.

Small teams can now produce the kind of work that used to require entire departments. A founder can move faster. A developer can build more. A marketer can create more content. A designer can explore more ideas. A business owner can automate work that used to consume hours every week.

This does not mean people no longer matter.

It means the right people matter more than ever.

The companies that win will be the ones that combine human taste, strategy, creativity, and judgment with intelligent systems that remove friction and multiply output.

That is the opportunity.

## What AI-Native Really Means

Being AI-native is not the same thing as occasionally using ChatGPT.

It means rethinking how work gets done.

It starts with better questions:

- Where are we repeating the same task every week?
- Where are we waiting on handoffs?
- Where are we manually creating things that could be generated, reviewed, and improved faster?
- Where are customers falling through the cracks?
- Where could software, automation, and AI agents give our team more leverage?

An AI-native company does not just add AI on top of old processes. It redesigns the process.

That might mean using AI to summarize calls, draft proposals, generate marketing content, create video scripts, produce social assets, analyze customer data, write internal documentation, automate onboarding, build dashboards, or assist with software development.

The goal is not to make people less important.

The goal is to help people spend more time on the work that actually matters.

## Why This Matters for Small Businesses

Large companies are already moving in this direction. They have internal teams, engineering resources, and budgets to experiment with AI workflows.

Small businesses need this too, maybe even more.

Most small businesses are not overstaffed. They are understaffed. They do not have giant marketing departments, software teams, or automation specialists. They have a few people trying to do everything.

That is where AI can be transformational.

A restaurant, nonprofit, event company, real estate firm, creative agency, retail brand, or local service business may not need a full internal AI department. But they do need better systems.

They need content created faster.
They need customer follow-up handled better.
They need websites and apps that actually support their operations.
They need videos, social posts, newsletters, and campaigns.
They need automations that save time.
They need tools that help their small team operate like a much larger one.

That is exactly where Next Wave Agentic fits.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

For a business, becoming AI-native can start small.

It might begin with an audit of current workflows. We look at how leads come in, how content gets created, how customers are followed up with, how the website works, how the team communicates, and where time is being wasted.

From there, we identify practical opportunities.

Maybe the business needs an AI-assisted content engine. Maybe it needs a better website. Maybe it needs a custom app. Maybe it needs internal automations. Maybe it needs a video pipeline that turns interviews, events, and product footage into consistent marketing content.

The key is that every recommendation should tie back to a business outcome.

- More content
- Faster delivery
- Better customer experience
- Less repetitive work
- More professional marketing
- Better internal visibility
- More revenue opportunities

AI by itself is not the goal.

Leverage is the goal.
